[arXiv260420] DataCenterGym: A Physics-Grounded Simulator for Multi-Objective Data Center Scheduling

arXiv

Authors
Nilavra Pathak, Samadrita Biswas, Nirmalya Roy
Institution
University of Maryland, Baltimore County, Expedia Group, Curia Global

The paper presents DataCenterGym, a physics-grounded simulator that integrates compute queueing and thermal dynamics for geo-distributed data centers, and develops a Hierarchical Model Predictive Control (H-MPC) scheduling algorithm. arXiv

Authors
Tuan Nguyen, Ting He
Institution
Pennsylvania State University

This paper proposes a method for optimizing the convergence time in decentralized federated learning by designing mixing matrices for Stochastic Gradient Push, which allows for asymmetric matrices and directed communication graphs. The core method involves analyzing the convergence rate to extract an objective function based on graph-theoretic parameters and developing an efficient design algorithm. The main conclusion is that the proposed solution significantly reduces convergence time compared to state-of-the-art methods without degrading model quality.

Institution

The paper presents BlockRaFT, a distributed framework that uses the RAFT consensus protocol to partition and coordinate stateful and stateless blockchain node operations across a cluster for improved scalability and fault tolerance.
