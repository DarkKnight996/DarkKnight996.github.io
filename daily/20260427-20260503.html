[arXiv260427] FlashSpread: IO-Aware GPU Simulation of Non-Markovian Epidemic Dynamics via Kernel Fusion

arXiv
sysGPU computingkernel fusion, CUDA Graphs, CSR traversal, degree-aware dispatch, mixed-precision, tau-leaping, non-Markovian simulation
Authors
Heman Shakeri, Behnaz Moradi-Jamei, Aram Vajdi, Ehsan Ardjmand
Institution
University of Virginia, James Madison University, Ohio University

FlashSpread is a GPU framework that accelerates non-Markovian epidemic simulation on large networks by fusing the entire renewal pipeline into a single Triton kernel, enabling intermediates to stay in registers. It achieves significant speedups over CPU implementations, scaling to 100 million nodes on a single A100, and maintains accuracy within typical epidemiological uncertainty.

[arXiv260427] Accelerating Intra-Node GPU-to-GPU Communication Through Multi-Path Transfers with CUDA Graphs

arXiv
syshigh-performance computingCUDA Graphs, UCX framework, multi-path communication, NVLink, PCIe, GPU-to-GPU communication, MPI
Authors
Amirhossein Sojoodi, Yiltan Hassan Temucin, Amirreza Baratisedeh, Hamed Sharifian, Ahmad Afsahi
Institution
Queen’s University

The paper proposes integrating CUDA Graphs into the UCX framework to enable concurrent multi-path point-to-point GPU communication within a node, leveraging NVLink and PCIe paths including host staging. This approach reduces communication overhead and launch costs by caching and reusing communication workflows as CUDA Graphs. Experimental results on a four-GPU node show up to a 2.95x bandwidth improvement over single-path UCX for message sizes up to 512MB.

[arXiv260427] Shard the Gradient, Scale the Model: Serverless Federated Aggregation via Gradient Partitioning

arXiv
mlsyscluster infrastructuregradient partitioning, serverless computing, federated learning, FedAvg, AWS Lambda
Authors
Amine Barrak
Institution
Oakland University

This paper proposes GradsSharding, a new serverless federated learning aggregation architecture that partitions the gradient tensor into shards, each averaged independently by a serverless function. This approach breaks the memory scalability ceiling of existing methods by bounding per-function memory independently of client count. Evaluations show GradsSharding achieves significant cost reductions and is the only architecture deployable beyond the serverless memory limit.

[arXiv260427] Data-Free Contribution Estimation in Federated Learning using Gradient von Neumann Entropy

arXiv
mlsysothersvon Neumann entropy, spectral entropy, data-free contribution estimation, Kalman filter, federated learning, aggregation
Authors
Asim Ukaye, Mubarak Abdu-Aguye, Nurbek Tastan, Karthik Nandakumar
Institution
MBZUAI, Michigan State University

This paper introduces a data-free metric called gradient von Neumann entropy to estimate client contributions in federated learning. The authors propose two practical schemes, SpectralFed and SpectralFuse, which use entropy for aggregation weighting and combine entropy with class-specific alignment for stability. Their experiments show that spectral entropy correlates highly with client accuracy across various non-IID benchmarks, serving as an effective indicator of client contribution without needing validation data.

[arXiv260427] -Approximation Coflow Scheduling in -Core Optical Circuit Switching Networks

arXiv
sysnetwork-schedulingcoflow scheduling, optical circuit switching, approximation algorithm, linear programming, multi-core networks
Authors
Xin Wang, Hong Shen, Hui Tian, Ye Tao
Institution
Central Queensland University, Griffith University

This paper proposes an algorithm for coflow scheduling in multi-core optical circuit switching (OCS) networks under an asynchronous reconfiguration model, combining linear programming-guided global ordering, inter-core flow allocation, and intra-core circuit scheduling. The algorithm achieves an 8K-approximation for zero release times and an (8K+1)-approximation for arbitrary release times, where K is the number of OCS cores, significantly improving prior results. Trace-driven experiments using Facebook workloads demonstrate strong practical performance in minimizing total weighted coflow completion time.

Authors
Jorge Villarrubia, Luis Costero, Francisco D. Igual, Katzalin Olcoz
Institution

This paper evaluates NVIDIA's MPS and MIG technologies for spatial GPU co-execution, finding that MPS offers flexibility but suffers from memory contention, while MIG provides hardware isolation with higher overhead. The study shows that MPS can improve performance by up to 30% and reduce energy by 20%, but may degrade performance by 30% under memory pressure, whereas MIG yields more consistent gains despite its rigidity.

[arXiv260427] Guess-Verify-Refine: Data-Aware Top-K for Sparse-Attention Decoding on Blackwell via Temporal Correlation

arXiv
mlsysGPU kernelssparse attention, Top-K selection, temporal correlation, Blackwell, TensorRT-LLM, DeepSeek Sparse Attention, Guess-Verify-Refine
Authors
Long Cheng, Ritchie Zhao, Timmy Liu, Mindy Li, Xianjie Qiao, Kefeng Duan, Yu-Jung Chen, Xiaoming Chen, Bita Darvish Rouhani, June Yang
Institution
NVIDIA

The paper proposes Guess-Verify-Refine (GVR), a data-aware exact Top-K algorithm for sparse-attention decoding on NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs that exploits temporal correlation across decode steps to accelerate key-value selection. GVR achieves an average 1.88x kernel speedup over the production radix-select kernel and up to 7.52% end-to-end TPOT improvement on DeepSeek-V3.2 workloads. The method preserves bit-exact outputs while reducing latency, with greater gains at longer contexts.

[arXiv260427] GICC: A High-Performance Runtime for GPU-Initiated Communication and Coordination in Modern HPC Systems

arXiv
sysHPC communication runtimeGPU-initiated communication, RDMA, LibFabric, SHMEM, one-sided communication, stencil computation, resource reclamation
Authors
Baodi Shan, Mauricio Araya-Polo, Barbara Chapman
Institution
Stony Brook University

GICC is a high-performance runtime that enables GPU kernels to directly trigger NIC-level operations for cross-node coordination without host involvement on the fast path, decoupling coordination semantics from data movement and introducing asynchronous resource reclamation. On Slingshot, it reduces per-coordination latency by up to 229x and improves weak scaling efficiency by up to 25%; on InfiniBand, it achieves up to 1.95x lower put latency than NVSHMEM. The framework demonstrates that GPU-driven, resource-aware coordination is achievable across modern HPC interconnects, improving parallel efficiency to 42% compared to MPI's 35.4% in an industrial stencil proxy.
